# Demo video — script + storyboard

Follow Section 7 of `voices-build-plan.docx`. Target: 60-second master; platform cuts derived from the master.

## Production notes (from the build plan)

- Shoot on a decent phone — yours is fine. Daylight if possible.
- Audio quality > video quality. Use a lav mic or phone mic in a quiet room; ambient room tone acceptable.
- Subtle ambient bed throughout the whole 60 s — no silent cuts between shots.
- Text overlays small and clean. Not TikTok-meme font. Cinematic restraint.
- Color-grade lightly toward warmth.
- No brand marks or copyrighted media in frame/audio (hackathon rule).

## Beats and timing — 60-second master

| Time | Shot | What happens | What the voice says |
|------|------|--------------|---------------------|
| 0–5 s | **Pigeon on sidewalk** | Phone pointed at pigeon. Photo taken. Greeting plays instantly. | "Crumbs, huh? That guy over there has a bagel. I'm calculating trajectories." |
| 5–12 s | Text overlay | "Everything has a voice." Fade. Ambient carries. | — |
| 12–20 s | **Houseplant** | Desk plant, afternoon light. Quick photo. | "I noticed you haven't been home much. Everything okay?" |
| 20–28 s | **Coffee mug + laptop** (same session) | A different moment, same user. Mug on laptop. | "Third one today. Worried about you." |
| 28–40 s | **Hero landmark — wide shot** | Wide of Rajwada / Taj Mahal / banyan tree. Phone capture. | "Quieter, once. The river came closer then. I could hear it at night." |
| 40–50 s | **Pair setup** | Candle in frame. Mirror added. User photographs both together. A pause. Then a third voice. | "You found me. What do you want to know?" |
| 50–60 s | Logo + tagline | Fade to dark. | "Overheard. The world has them if you listen." |

## Hook lines for the first 3 seconds (choose one)

- "I built an app where you can talk to anything."
- "Everything in your house has an opinion about you. Now you can hear them."
- "Took a photo of a pigeon. The pigeon had thoughts."
- "The Taj Mahal spoke to me in first person. Here's what it said."
- "Photograph two things together. Sometimes a third voice shows up."

## Backup shots (film opportunistically)

- Empty-plate + fork after lunch
- Pen + blank paper on a desk
- Key + door at the office entrance
- Ring + letter on a nightstand
- Two books stacked on a side table

Any of these can substitute for the primary pair-unlock moment if the candle-mirror shot misfires.

## Must-haves before publishing

- [ ] Every spoken voice is crystal clear — no clipping, no hiss
- [ ] Ambient bed is present but under -18 dB vs. the greeting voice
- [ ] No third-party trademarks, logos, or copyrighted music visible/audible
- [ ] Tight outro card with the tagline and no URL (URLs in description)
- [ ] Uploaded to YouTube, public (or unlisted acceptable per rules)
